🎓 Graduation cap

The ETH a bonding curve collects before it graduates to Uniswap — and roughly the DEX liquidity each token launches with. A bigger cap = thicker pool, better on DexScreener, less slippage. The virtual reserves are rescaled with the cap (virtualEth = cap × 1122 ⁄ 2600) so the curve shape — price multiple and % of supply sold — stays identical; only the ETH denomination changes. Applies to new launches only. setCurveParams((uint128,uint128,uint128))

💰 Claim launchpad revenue

All protocol fees — deploy fees, the 1% curve fee, and the 10% protocol share of creator levies — accrue as pending revenue. Claim sends it to your treasury (or, once set, the staking vault). Sweep rescues any stray ETH to the treasury and can never touch creator balances or pending revenue. Both are safe anytime. flushProtocol() · sweepUnaccounted()

📊 Fee model reference

How the launchpad earns — and what you can change. The split percentages are fixed in the contract as a fair-launch guarantee; only the deploy fee is tunable.
Deploy fee— per launch → 100% launchpad · settable
Curve trade fee1% of every bonding-curve trade → 100% launchpad · fixed
Creator levyup to 10% buy/sell (creator sets per token) → 90% creator / 10% launchpad · fixed split
Graduation100% of LP burned · no protocol cut · fixed

⚠️ Ownership (danger zone)

The factory and fee router use 2-step ownership: transferOwnership names a pending owner, then that wallet must call acceptOwnership — so a typo can't lock you out. Transfer moves the factory; remember to transfer the fee router too if you're rotating keys. Renounce is irreversible and permanently freezes all controls above.
